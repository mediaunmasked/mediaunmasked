50+ Sites For Royalty Free Music And Sound Effects

TL;DR: Here’s a big list I’ve compiled of sites that mostly offer free music or sound effects that you can use, royalty-free, in your videos or other projects.

The following is a collective list of all the sites I’ve found that offer music and sound effects for video production. They are in no specific order. Most of them are royalty free and require no attribution, but there are some that do require specific details, so be sure to read the licensing details for each site before you use it for commercial purposes. <div class="clearfix"></div>

50. YouTube

That's right, YouTube's new audio library is specifically designed for creators in mind of both video and music. So if you're looking to make a video with some free music, you can choose from the library. Although not many songs are in it right now, music makers can upload their work to add to the project.

Style: Calm and easy for voice over and miscellaneous videos.

49. LDUK Music (On YouTube)

If dubsteps your genre, Luke Dalton from the UK has a music channel that features many famous pop culture such as Harry Potter, GTAV and Call of Duty Zombie remixes.

Style: Dubstep

48. Jojikiba (On YouTube)

Jojikiba puts up sound effects that may be very useful in specific videos that need some extra soundscape such as an animation.

Style: Sound Effects

47. Sound Effects Factor (On YouTube)

Sound Effects Factory has a large sound effects library that is constantly being update.

Style: Sound Effects

46. Activity Music (On YouTube)

Activity Music is another YouTube-based music channel that uploads upbeat genres like electro, dubsteb and hard dance, among others.

Style: Dubstep, house, hard dance, electro, trance, other

45. Free Sound

Search from a ton of sound effects based on any keyword at freesound.org.

Style: Sound Effects

44. Flash Kit

Flash Kit offers sound effects that are categorised in groups that would be relevant to your work.

Style: Sound effects

43. Find Sounds

This useful search engine searches the web for different sound effects that you can further filter based on the format, channels, resolution and sample rate.

Style: Sound Effects

42. Newgrounds

Newgrounds has a large community of music makers that put their stuff online so it gets used and they get noticed. Most of it has special licensing agreements, but you can always contact the creator and make our some sort of deal; most people are pretty willing to help.

Style: All genres of music

41. Incompetech

Incompetech is one of the most well-known sites for royalty-free music. For this reason, many of the tracks are recognised by some people, possibly detracting from the video, so keep that possibility in mind. Also, the artist puts this music online for you to use under the Creative Commons by Attribution license – meaning you must give him credit when used.

Style: Varied

40. Dig CC Mixter

Dig CC Mixter can sort some music options with commercial use and for what medium you're using it for.

Style: Coffee shop, podcast, video, etc.

39. 1Soundfx

1Soundfx is another large assortment of sound effects, but this site is not free, so I recommend only using this one if you're out of other options.

Style: Sound Effects

38. Danosongs

Danosongs has been around for a while. It's just one guy who makes music that you can use royalty free, so you should donate so he can continue it.

Style: Varied

37. Audio Micro

Audio Micro is a stock library for audio which includes royalty free music and sound effects.

Style: Sound Effects

36. Jamendo

Jamendo offers over 400,000 tracks, but they do cost some money. They won't break the bank, so they could be a viable option.

Style: Varied

35. Square Peach Music

Square Peach Music offers an unlimited use license for $79 if you like and plan to use their music for a while.

Style: Varied

34. Smart Sound

Smart Sound has some nice variety of royalty free music such as jazz, rock, urban, orchestral and more that you can use for a video or other project.

Style: Varied

33. Partners In Rhymes

Yet another site that offers royalty free music: Partners In Rhymes.

Style: Varied, sound effects

32. Free Play Music

Targeted towards YouTube videos, Free Play Music has a nice selection of tracks to pick from.

Style: Varied

31. Smart Sound + Incompetech

This little tool lets you pick some music from Incompetech, but filter it by how long your video is in intervals of small amounts of time. The tracks were created at different durations knowing that people might be using them to make intros or other short videos.

Style: Varied

30. Shockwave-Sound

Even lesser-known sites like shockwave-sound might surprise you with some of the tracks; plus, they'll set your music apart from others who use common sites like Incompetech.

Style: Stock music, varied, sound effects

29. Royalty Free Music Library

Royalty free is in the name, so you know it must be royalty free.

Style: Varied

28. Soungle

Soungle is a search engine for sound.

Style: Sound effects

27. Sound Jay

Sound Jay's sound effects library is royalty free and costs nothing to you. But you can't take advantage of the creators by reselling them or other nonsense.

Style: Sound effects

26. Sound Bible

Sound Bible has free sound clips, bites and effects.

Style: Sound effects

25. Machinimasound

Machinimasound has songs that they encourage people to use in their machinimas (machinima = a portmanteau of machine and cinema).

Style: Varied

24. Beats Royalty Free

Another browse-by-genre royalty free music website.

Style: Varied

23. iStockPhoto

iStockPhoto isn't only for photos. It also features illustrations, video and audio.

Style: Varied

22. Sound Snap

SoundSnap has sound effects and some music. They offer a $249/year price for unlimited download; a bit pricey, but cheaper than hiring a specialist for sound design.

Style: Sound effects, loops

21. Free Music Archive

While still in beta, Free Music Archive has a nice curation of content that can be filtered by genre or curator.

Style: Varied

20. Pond5

Pond5 is another stock media site that contains more than music.

Style: Varied

19. Moby Gratis

Moby Gratis is a composer who makes music with independent filmmakers in mind.

Style: Cinematic

18. Jonathan Coulton

From the guy who brought you Re: Your Brains, Jonathan Coulton offers his music on his website for free/cheap download.

Style: Varied, fun

17. Josh Woodward

Josh Woodward offers creative commons "songs for people who like things." I like things, don't you?

Style: Varied

16. Free Stock Music

Free stock music offers 100% free production music for anything your little heart desires.

Style: Stock music, varied

15. Oxygen Star Power

This hidden site was found in the corner of the internet sitting around; do with it what you want.

Style: Tech

14. Free Soundtrack Music

Free soundtrack music is like the other music curation sites, it's filterable by genre.

Style: Varied

13. PacDV

PacDV has a bulk sound effects download available, but they ask that you attribute them by giving a link back to their site when you use their music.

Style: Sound effects

12. Public Domain 4U

Public Domain 4U offers some good ol' public domain tracks.

Style: Early 1900s, varies, public domain

11. Musopen

Musopen offers a similar, public domain, music choices from composers such as Bach, Beethoven, Borodin and Schubert.

Style: Public domain, old, varied

10. Beatpick

Beatpick has a selection of music that can be used in non-commercial or non-profit video productions.

Style: Varied

9. Royalty Free Pianos

Looking for some nice, royalty free, piano music for you production? Royalty Free Pianos has a nice selection to choose from.

Style: Piano, easy, cinematic

8. Tune Society

Tune Society offers a license for royalty free music as low as $30. Again, there are plenty of free alternatives, so only use a paid site if you absolutely have to have a song they offer or their style fits yours as a videomaker.

Style: Varied

7. Premium Beat

Premium beat has a one time fee to enable you to use all of their tracks, both much and sound effects, forever with 100% copyright clear.

Style: Varied

6. Sonnyboo

Sonnyboo has royalty free tracks that are also free to download. However, the tracks are licensed under the Creative Commons by Attribution license.

Style: Varied

5. Soundclick

Soundclick is another site that has a wide variety which can come at a cost.

Style: Varied

4. Derek Audette

Derek Audette offers all of his songs in one zip file for your convenience.

Style: Creepy

3. Lino Rise

Lino Rise, from Germany, offers his creative work for free on his website.

Style: Varied

2. Sound Cloud

Soundcloud is a well-known HUB for musicians to create and share their work. While most of it is protected under copyright, some artists choose to let others use their music – sometimes under specific requirements like attribution. You can search Soundcloud for music licensed under creative commons so you can use it in your production.

Style: All

1. Melody Loops

Melody loops is a very useful site to get music if you're looking for some background music that should be able to repeat for as long as you'd like it to. They offer some quality music, but they're not free. Actually each license is pretty expencive from $5 to $10, they aren't cheap, but they do sound nice.

Style: Easy, background, varied
